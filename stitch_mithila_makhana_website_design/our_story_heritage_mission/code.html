A Millennium of Tradition

For over a thousand years, the wetlands of Mithila have nurtured the Gorgon Nut, famously known as Makhana. This isn't just a snack; it's a cultural anchor for the region, celebrated in ancient Vedic texts and preserved by generations of farming communities.

Our Makhana holds the prestigious Geographical Indication (GI) Tag, ensuring that every seed you consume is grown in the mineral-rich waters of North Bihar using the exact methods perfected by our ancestors.

From Pond to Palate

Our four-step artisanal roasting process preserves maximum nutrition while creating the signature crunch.

Hand Harvesting

Seeds are manually collected from the pond floor—a labor-intensive process requiring incredible skill and breath control.

Iron Pot Roasting

Seeds are roasted in traditional cast-iron pots over controlled flames. This double-roasting method is crucial for the expansion of the seeds.

02. ROASTING

Manual Threshing

Individual seeds are hand-struck at the precise moment of expansion to break the black shell and reveal the white core.
